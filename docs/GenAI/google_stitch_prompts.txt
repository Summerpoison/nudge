Hey, please create a prototype for a productivity tool called "Nudge". 

The tool itself is focused on the idea that tasks/to-do's are dumped into it and the tool then does the thinking on priotization, based on the deadline. At certain intervalls (eg 50% of deadline time passed) the tool does a "check in" with the user, who then needs to submit proof they worked/are working on the task. The user can skip this, but needs to provide a reason. The system offers an e-mail draft depending on the reason (eg help request to designated coworker) to make completing the task easier/achivable.

The MVP loop is:
1. Task exists with a real deadline
2. System auto-calculates buffer deadline and checkpoints
3. At checkpoint: show proof OR tell me what's blocking you (triage menu)
4. System drafts whatever message the triage answer implies (help request, extension, follow-up)
5. You hit send

Visually Nudge is centered around a minimalistic and clean aestethic. It should feel light and airy with clear to-do markers and an easy way to switch views -- no complex menus and intentional elements.
